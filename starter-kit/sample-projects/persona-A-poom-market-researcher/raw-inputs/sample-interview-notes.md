# Interview Notes: Thailand Digital Payment Adoption 2025

## Interview 1: Niran Chaisiri, Head of Digital Products, Krittaya Retail Bank (mid-size Thai retail bank)
- Customers age 45+ now at 58% monthly mobile app active rate, up from 41% in early 2023
- "QR is now normal for daily spend, but first-time setup still scares older customers"
- Preferred channels by 45+ segment: bank app QR 46%, prompt transfer 31%, debit card online 15%, cash-on-delivery fallback 8%
- Onboarding friction: ID verification drop-off at selfie step, 22% fail to complete first attempt
- Branch-assisted onboarding improved completion from 78% to 91% in pilot across 12 branches
- Compliance constraint: higher scrutiny for remote onboarding since 2024 fraud controls update, extra checks add 2-3 minutes per flow
- Regulatory mention: e-KYC threshold rules force manual review for edge cases, especially inconsistent name spellings

## Interview 2: Pailin Rattanakul, Operations Manager, MailyCart (mid-size Thai e-commerce company)
- Payment preference by segment: Gen Z wallet 52%, millennials mixed wallet/card 49/28, age 45+ bank transfer 44%
- Cart abandonment overall 67%; when gateway response slower than 4 seconds, abandonment rises to 74%
- "Customers do not complain about price first, they complain when payment looks uncertain"
- One-click stored card users convert at 2.1x compared with manual card entry
- Cash-on-delivery still 19% for outer provinces, but down from 27% in 2022
- API integration pain point: different retry logic across providers, reconciliation team spends 6-8 hours weekly on mismatch cleanup
- After adding backup payment route in checkout, payment failure rate dropped from 3.9% to 1.6%

## Interview 3: Dr. Kanya Vejjajiva, Financial Inclusion Researcher, Center for Inclusive Finance Policy (Thai think tank)
- Estimated unbanked adults in Thailand at 6.2 million, concentrated in informal workers and remote rural groups
- Rural mobile wallet adoption reached 49% in 2024 survey, up from 34% in 2021
- Provincial gap remains: Bangkok metro digital payment usage 81%, selected rural provinces 46-55%
- "Trust and agent support matter as much as smartphone access"
- Regional comparison: Vietnam strong merchant QR push in tier-2 cities; Indonesia agent networks reduced onboarding fear for first-time users
- Policy pattern that worked: local language onboarding guides plus village-level digital payment ambassadors, pilot showed +14 percentage point adoption in 9 months
- Recommendation: focus funding on assisted onboarding and merchant incentive design, not only app feature expansion
